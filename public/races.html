Not all types of bike races look like the Tour de France. Check out the types of races we love to ride.



Road

Fast and Furious

Road racing is on smooth paved roads or trails. Criteriums (Crits) consist of riding laps around a short circuit. Cyclists often ride closely together and use drafting to increase their speeds. Some crits also have “sprint” laps which earn the fastest racers extra points during that lap. Time trials are another popular road race, where each individual races the clock. In this type of race drafting is not permitted.

Our favorites: Machinery Hill Crits, SouthSide Sprints


Cyclocross

Obstacle course on a bike

Typically raced in the fall, cylocross (CX), is an off-road race, which can include sections of grass, dirt, mud, gravel, pavement, and sand. The races are based on a set time (typically 25 to 60 minutes), where you race as many laps as you can on the course. CX races can include obstacles like jumps, stairs, and sand pits where you may choose to ‘hike a bike’

Our favorites: Fulton Star Cross, Baker Orchard Donkey Cross


Triathlons

Swim, Bike, Run

Triathlons consist of three sections, swimming, biking, then running. Tri’s can range from shorter sprint distances (0.5mi swim/12.4mi bike/3.1mi run) to endurance Ironman (2.4mi swim/112 mi bike/26.2mi run). Relay tri’s are a team event, where each section is raced by a different athlete.

Our favorites: Lake Minnetonka Triathlon, YWCA Women’s Tri


Gravel

Scenic, unpaved adventure

Gravel races are a great way to shake up your riding and explore more rural areas. Often on unmaintained country roads, gravel races are typically longer rides, and are based on milage, not laps.

Our favorites: Riot Grrravel, Filthy 50, Miesville 56


Fat Tire

Winter fun

Winters can get long in Minnesota, but Fat Tire races are a way to have cycling fun all year long. Typically raced January through March, Fat Tire races are done on set courses of groomed snow. The courses may include elements of singletrack or jumps to keep it exciting.

Our favorites: Fat Tire Loppet, Fat Wednesdays


Mountain

Shredding Singletrack

Mountain Bike (MTB) races are off-road races on rough terrain, often on fire roads, single or doubletrack. Obstacles on this type of race may include roots, rocks, jumps, switchbacks, and hills.

Our favorites: Lutsen 99er, Thursday Night Buck Hill
